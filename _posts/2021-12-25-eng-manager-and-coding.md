I have been engineering manager for 6-7 years, however I spent the last year as IC, but recently
switched back to management when I joined Booking. The expectations around and engineering manager
never been clear, especially when it comes to coding. It was always flavoured by the organisation
where I was manager. It was always simple but still complex situation. I believe, the industry also
shifts to a more structured way of doing things. I'm not sure whether we can say that it is more
mature compared to what we had 5 years ago, but still it seems the extreme attitudes and setups are
less and less.

Long story short, in my opinion an engineering manager should write code. If it is possible it
should be production code. But, there are things need to be considered.

Engineering management is about delivery, technology and people in the same time. Delivery includes
the "what?", "when? (deadlines)", "how frequently?" and "method". The technology includes the "tech
stack", "architecture", "tech debt" and "quality" stuff. People includes
"motivation", "career development", "goals", "responsibilities", "team morale" and "work-life
balance" aspects. There is a fine balance - which change company by company - which results in a
software delivery. The task of the engineering manager is keep the things in balance so the team can
deliver. Writing code is not part of the above. Where it is then? It is part of the trust which is
an ingredient of all the above.

When it is about delivery the word "clarity" is mentioned lots of times. It means everyone in the
situation is aware of what, when and in what pace is going to happen, and what does it look like
when it is done. It sounds easy, however in a software delivery, in general, multiple fields are
connected. Software engineer works with designers, accountants, lawyers, human behavior experts,
finance people and so on. They speak a different language. "Clarity" means when finance says
something related to the delivery then it means the same for software engineers as it does for
finance folks. This part of the job is extremely hard. Engineering manager is responsible and
accountable for ensuring the "clarity" in terms of delivery. He/she is the mediator between
different fields. As she/he is doing this a certain amount of trust between the people is going to
build up.
